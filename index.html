Hipertensi

a medical condition in which blood pressure in the body's arteries is persistently elevated. High blood pressure can increase the risk of various serious health problems such as heart attack, stroke, kidney failure and other health problems. Hypertension can be caused by various factors including an unhealthy lifestyle, genetic factors, obesity, high salt consumption, and other factors

a condition in which blood pressure in the body's arteries persistently increases and can increase the risk of serious health problems.

Diagnosa hypertension

To diagnose hypertension, blood pressure must be measured regularly and if systolic blood pressure (blood pressure when the heart contracts) is above 140 mmHg or diastolic blood pressure (blood pressure when the heart is at rest) is above 90 mmHg, then a person can be classified as suffering from hypertension

Management of hypertension

Management of hypertension involves lifestyle changes such as a healthy diet, regular exercise, reducing salt consumption, and avoiding smoking. Additionally, in some cases, doctors can also prescribe medications to help lower blood pressure.

Some examples of foods that can increase the risk of hypertension include: 1.Foods high in salt: Processed foods, fast foods, canned foods, and ready-to-eat foods often contain high levels of salt which can increase blood pressure. 2.Foods high in saturated fat: Foods high in saturated fat such as fatty red meat, fast food, fried foods, and high-fat dairy products can increase the risk of hypertension.

Diabetes millitus

Diabetes mellitus is a medical condition characterized by high blood sugar levels due to impaired insulin metabolism

In people with diabetes mellitus, the body cannot produce enough insulin or cannot use insulin effectively, so blood sugar levels become high.

Symptoms of diabetes mellitus include frequent thirst, frequent urination, unexplained weight loss, fatigue, and difficult wounds.

To keep the body health

To keep your body healthy, there are several things you need to pay attention to. First, it is important to maintain a balanced diet by consuming nutritious foods such as vegetables, fruit, protein and complex carbohydrates. Second, diligently exercise regularly to maintain physical fitness and heart health. Third, make sure to get enough rest every night so that the body has time to recover and repair. Fourth, avoid smoking and excessive alcohol consumption because it can have a bad impact on health. Apart from that, it is also important to maintain personal hygiene and the surrounding environment to avoid infectious diseases. Always wash your hands before eating and after using the toilet, and regularly clean your house and residence. Lastly, don't forget to carry out regular health checks and follow the doctor's recommendations to maintain overall health. By maintaining a healthy diet, exercising regularly, getting enough rest, avoiding bad habits, maintaining cleanliness, and having regular health checks, the body can stay healthy and fit.
